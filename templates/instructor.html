Instructor Guide

Start with solo mode

  1. Students play once first in solo mode to get used to the mechanics and explore how a sole owner might manage the aquifer. They choose one pumping decision for the six wells in the aquifer.
  2. Then, ask students to form groups of six. (Groups of five are fine. Four is okay, not great.) Ask one student in each group to act as host, which just means starting a room for their group.
  3. Students navigate to the group mode webpage. The host creates a room and shares the room code with their groupmates. *If the group is less than six people, they should change the group size setting before starting the room*.
  4. The game auto-advances once all students in a group have submitted their pumping decision. The game ends after 8 rounds.

Game Flow

Each round:

  1. Students choose their pumping rate (0 to qmax)
  2. When all students submit, the round automatically advances
  3. Results update in real-time for everyone

Host controls:

  • Force Advance: Move to next round without waiting for all submissions
  • End Game Now: Finish immediately and display final results

Key Parameters

Essential parameters to understand:

  • S₀ (Initial Stock): Starting aquifer level
  • R (Recharge): Natural replenishment per round
  • T (Rounds): Game length (default: 8)
  • qmax (Max Pumping): Maximum extraction per player per round
